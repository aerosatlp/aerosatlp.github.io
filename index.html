About Project

Aerosat Launch Program is Satellite Launch Project designed by a group of Undergraduate students. Our Mission is to send a satellite to high altitude in atmosphere and study the atmospheric data. We have developed a satellite less with sensors and modules. This Project includes acquisition of atmospheric data, study the stratosphere, capture images.

Raspberry Pi

The Raspberry Pi is a compact single-board computer renowned for its affordability and versatility. Developed by the Raspberry Pi Foundation, features a quad-core ARM Cortex-A72 processor and supports up to 4GB or 8GB of RAM. With Gigabit Ethernet, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth connectivity, along with USB ports, HDMI output, and a microSD card slot, it provides a robust platform for a variety of projects. The GPIO pins enable hardware interfacing, making it a popular choice for hobbyists, educators, and professionals exploring applications ranging from home automation to programming education.

Lora Technology

LoRa (Long Range) technology is a wireless communication protocol designed for long-range, low-power applications in the Internet of Things (IoT). It operates in the sub-gigahertz frequency bands, offering extended communication ranges with minimal power consumption. LoRaWAN, a key application of LoRa technology, enables secure and efficient communication between devices, making it a popular choice for IoT deployments in smart cities, agriculture, and industrial settings.

Camera & Sensors

A Raspberry Pi can be integrated with cameras and sensors to simulate satellite functions. Cameras, like the Raspberry Pi Camera Module, provide imaging capabilities, capturing visual data for Earth observation or educational purposes. Sensors, such as accelerometers, gyroscopes, or environmental sensors, can be added to gather information on the simulated satellite's orientation, movement, or environmental conditions.
